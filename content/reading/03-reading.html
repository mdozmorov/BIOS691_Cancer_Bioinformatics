References

  • Milestones in Genomic Sequencing by Nature, 2000-2021 period, interactive infographics

  • Claussnitzer, Melina, Judy H. Cho, Rory Collins, Nancy J. Cox, Emmanouil T. Dermitzakis, Matthew E. Hurles, Sekar Kathiresan, et al. “A Brief History of Human Disease Genetics.” Nature, (January 2020) - Timeline and milestones in genomics development focusing on disease genetics.

  • Hagen, J. B. “The Origins of Bioinformatics.” Nature Reviews. Genetics, (01 December 2000)

  • The (near) complete sequence of a human genome - efforts for completing human genome assembly

  • Mardis, Elaine R. “DNA Sequencing Technologies: 2006-2016.” Nature Protocols, (February 2017) - DNA sequencing technologies introduction, references.

  • Heather, James M., and Benjamin Chain. “The Sequence of Sequencers: The History of Sequencing DNA.” Genomics, (January 2016) - Review of sequencing technologies, from pre-Sanger to current PacBio, Oxford Nanopore, Ion Torrent.

  • Goodwin, Sara, John D. McPherson, and W. Richard McCombie. “Coming of Age: Ten Years of next-Generation Sequencing Technologies.” Nature Reviews. Genetics, (17 2016) - From microarrays to long read sequencing, single-cell sequencing. Technology review. Table with all technologies and costs. Figures.

  • Green, Eric D., Mark S. Guyer, Eric D. Green, Mark S. Guyer, Teri A. Manolio, and Jane L. Peterson. “Charting a Course for Genomic Medicine from Base Pairs to Bedside.” Nature, (February 10, 2011) - Perspective on genomics development, technologies. Figure 1 - pictorial roadmap

  • Mardis, Elaine R. “Next-Generation DNA Sequencing Methods.” Annual Review of Genomics and Human Genetics, (2008) - Sequencing technologies review. DNA-/RNA-/ChIP-seq. Figures.

  • E. S. Lander et al., “Initial Sequencing and Analysis of the Human Genome,” Nature, (February 15, 2001) - Genome sequencing landmark paper.

Genomics resources

  • SequencEnG - An interactive learning resource for next-generation sequencing (NGS) techniques. Each technology had the corresponding reference and infographics.

  • Links and references to many resources by Ming Tang

  • Bioinformatics-Resources - A curated list of resources for learning bioinformatics.

  • biotools - A massive collection of references on the topics of bioinformatics, sequencing technologies, programming, machine learning, and more.

  • Computational genomics with R book by Altuna Akalin. GitHub repo
